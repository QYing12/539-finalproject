Plan Your Visit

Curated routes to experience the essence of Suzhou's classical gardens

Essential Tips

Best Time to Visit

Spring (April–May) for flowers, Autumn (October–November) for foliage. Avoid summer heat and winter cold.

Photography

Early morning light is best. Bring a wide-angle lens for architecture and a telephoto for details.

Pace Yourself

Garden appreciation requires calm and time. Plan no more than 3 gardens per day.

Getting Around

Most gardens are within walking distance or a short taxi ride. The metro connects major sites.

Classic Route

Full Day

Experience the three most iconic gardens, each representing different periods and styles of Suzhou garden art.

9:00 AM 2.5 hours

Humble Administrator's Garden

Arrive early to avoid crowds. Begin at the Eastern Garden and move west. Don't miss the Hall of Distant Fragrance.

12:00 PM 1 hour

Lunch at Pingjiang Road

Try local specialties like Squirrel-Shaped Mandarin Fish or biluochun tea.

1:30 PM 1.5 hours

Lion Grove Garden

Famous for its labyrinthine rockery. Take time to explore the rock caves and find the hidden stone lions.

4:00 PM 1.5 hours

Master of the Nets Garden

Best visited in late afternoon. If possible, attend the evening classical music performance (seasonal).

Contemplative Route

Half Day

A more intimate journey focused on detailed appreciation of garden elements and quiet reflection.

9:00 AM 2.5 hours

Lingering Garden

Move slowly through the covered walkways. Study each framed view. The garden rewards patient observation.

12:00 PM 1 hour

Tea at Garden Pavilion

Enjoy a traditional tea ceremony in a garden setting. Reflect on the morning's experience.

1:30 PM 1.5 hours

Couple's Retreat Garden

One of the smaller gardens. Perfect for understanding how Chinese gardens create vastness in limited space.
